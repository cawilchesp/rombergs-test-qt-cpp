5. DATA DISPLAY

To visualize the results, the buttons New Study and Multiple Analysis are used. A new window will open (see Figure 3), where the data to be ANALYZED is chosen. In the left list, there are the patient's folders or data text files, which are in the data folder defined in settings.

Figura 3. New Study.

The data files are selected and added to the right list, with the button ▶. The files on the right list will be those that will be analyzed in the new study. To remove files from the right list, they are selected and the button ◀ is pressed. With the button New Study, when multiple files are selected, the data is processed so the average of the selected signals is shown, while with the button Multiple Analysis, each file is processed individually.

Finally, it is necessary to give a name to the study. When completed, the button Add Study will be enabled, and when pressed, the results of the study will be presented in the main window (see Figure 4).

Figura 4. Visualization of the results.

5.1 Signals

Lateral and antero-posterior oscillation signals are presented from the selected data. In these windows, all the studies that the user generates are presented.

Figura 5. Presentation of lateral and antero-posterior oscillation signals.

5.2 Areas

The location of the selected data is presented and the areas of the regions that contain the data are defined. The regions are defined by three methods:

  1. Ellipse: The ellipse containing the data, with horizontal and vertical axes, is calculated from the maximum and minimum values.
  2. Convex hull: The convex hull with minimum area is calculated to contain all the data.
  3. Oriented ellipse: The ellipse is calculated with axes oriented to contain most of the data.

Figura 6. Presentación de áreas de oscilación.

5.3 Parameters

From research in scientific literature, the parameters of stabilometry that are analyzed in the study are defined:

Parameter Description Calculation
Oscillation range It is the difference between the maximum and minimum values of lateral or antero-posterior oscillation.

VMAX: Maximum value

VMIN: Minimum value
Mean velocity (MVML,AP) The mean velocity is the sum of the velocities at each moment of time, the barycenter.

xi: Magnitude in an instant i.

ti: Time instant.

n: Number of points.
Total mean velocity (MV) The total mean velocity is the displacement of the barycenter, both in x and in y, in the total analysis time.

AP and ML: Signals of antero-posterior and lateral oscillation, respectively.

N: Total number of points of the signal.

T: Total analysis time.
Signal RMS The average distance between the barycenter and each point of the signal.

Si: Point of the signal.

S̄: Average of the signal.

N: Number of data in the signal.
RMS distance (DRMS) Represents the standard distribution of the data.

Mean distance (MD) It is the average distance of the COP path.

Mean frequency (MF) Is the number of revolutions per second, in an uniform circular motion with radius MD, and its path is the total path of the COP.

Area of balance by limits (A) The radii of the ellipse are calculated from the maximums and minimums of the signals.

Area of balance by convex hull The area of the minimum polygon that encloses all points.
Area of balance by principal components analysis (PCA) The eigenvectors of the points of the signal are calculated. The first eigenvector defines the radii of the ellipse and its angle of rotation.
